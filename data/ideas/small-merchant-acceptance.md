## small-merchant-acceptance

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| slug | small-merchant-acceptance |
| idea | Build the small merchant bitcoin acceptance into a simplified experience. |
| card_body | Most bitcoin wallets give merchants a receive screen. A real point of sale has preset items, a keypad, payment history, and clean accounting export. Today's options are self-hosted server setups, custodial mobile apps, or scattered features. Ship the open self-hostable cohesive mobile POS. |
| body | Most bitcoin wallets give merchants a receive screen. That's not a POS. A POS has preset items, a keypad, notes, payment history, and a way to export it all. [BTCPay Server](https://btcpayserver.org/) has this but requires hosting your own server. [Wallet of Satoshi](https://www.walletofsatoshi.com/) and [Breez](https://breez.technology/) have it as mobile apps, but neither tracks payment rails or exports clean accounting data. [Square](https://squareup.com/us/en/press/block-to-roll-out-bitcoin-payments-on-square) shipped Lightning acceptance to its merchant base in 2025 (custodial-via-Square). [Coinsnap](https://coinsnap.io/pos/) ships a self-custodial mobile POS. The open self-hostable cohesive stack (preset items, keypad, price display in customer currency, clean accounting export, no server keys) doesn't exist. |
